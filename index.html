About me

My name is Bradie Fernandez, and for as long as I can remember, I’ve been fascinated by programming. I’ve always been eager to learn the ins and outs of how developers think and solve problems. My programming journey began in high school with a C++ course, which opened my eyes to the unlimited possibilities and techniques within the world of coding.

I continued my journey at Penn State University, where I earned my Bachelor of Science in the College of Information Sciences and Technology. During my time at Penn State, I expanded my skills in areas like networking, API integration, and user experience (UX) design. A major highlight was my internship at Penn State Hershey Medical Center, where I had the opportunity to work on a heart disease app that delivered patient information and provided doctors with valuable feedback. Our four-person team spent nearly a year building the app, and it was by far the best learning experience I’ve had so far. Through this project, I learned valuable skills such as coding in React.js and applying Test-Driven Development (TDD) to fix bugs and improve the app’s performance.

Since graduating, I’ve continued to hone my programming skills by revisiting the basics and steadily building more complex projects. I’m passionate about sharpening my abilities, learning new technologies, and creating solutions that have a real-world impact.
